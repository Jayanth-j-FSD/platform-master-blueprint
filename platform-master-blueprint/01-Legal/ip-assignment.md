# INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

**This Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement (“Agreement”) is made on [Date], by and between:**

**1. [Founder/Contributor Name] (“Assignor”)**  
**2. [Company Name] (“Company”)**, a company to be incorporated / already incorporated.

Assignor and Company collectively referred to as the “Parties.”

---

# 1. Purpose of This Agreement

The Assignor is contributing to the development of a unified digital platform consisting of:

- Identity & verification systems  
- Social graph infrastructure  
- Marketplace logic  
- Collaboration tools  
- Payment systems  
- Algorithms, research, design, and all related technology  

This Agreement ensures **all intellectual property (“IP”) created by Assignor belongs fully and exclusively to the Company.**

---

# 2. Assignment of Intellectual Property

The Assignor hereby **irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys** to the Company:

### **All rights, titles, and interests in any IP created by the Assignor**, including but not limited to:

- Source code (any language)  
- System architecture  
- API designs  
- Models, algorithms, schemas  
- UI/UX designs, graphics, components  
- Documents, planning materials, diagrams  
- Research, analyses, whitepapers  
- Names, branding, logos, design marks  
- Database schemas and engineered data structures  
- Business logic, workflows, technical strategies  
- Proprietary tools, scripts, utilities, libraries  
- Presentations, pitches, investor docs  
- Development environments and configurations  
- Any improvements, modifications, or reworks of existing IP  
- Any IP created using Company resources  

This assignment includes **copyrights, patents, trademarks, trade secrets**, and **moral rights**.

Assignor grants the Company **worldwide, perpetual, exclusive ownership** of all IP.

---

# 3. Work-for-Hire (If Applicable)

To the extent any IP created by the Assignor qualifies as “work made for hire” under applicable law:

- It is agreed that the IP **shall be deemed created for the Company**,  
- And the Company shall be considered the **author and owner** from the moment of creation.

---

# 4. No Retained Rights

Assignor confirms:

- They retain **no rights, ownership, or control** over any IP created for the Company.  
- They cannot reuse, license, copy, or distribute any of the IP outside Company authorization.  
- They cannot claim credit, authorship rights, or demand for usage restrictions.

---

# 5. Future Intellectual Property

All IP created **in the future** during engagement with the Company is **automatically assigned** to the Company without requiring additional agreements.

This includes:

- New products  
- Enhancements  
- Features  
- Algorithms  
- Tools created during employment or contribution

---

# 6. Confidentiality Obligations

The Assignor agrees not to disclose or misuse:

- Source code  
- Architecture diagrams  
- Identity graph logic  
- Marketplace algorithms  
- System configurations  
- Financial models  
- Roadmaps  
- Internal communications  
- Any undisclosed development plans  

Confidentiality applies **during and after** the Assignor’s relationship with the Company.

---

# 7. Representations & Warranties

The Assignor warrants that:

1. All IP created is original and does not violate any third-party rights.  
2. No part of the IP was copied or taken from previous employment or other sources.  
3. The Assignor has full rights to assign the IP to the Company.  
4. The IP is free from claims, liens, or encumbrances.  
5. The Assignor will assist in resolving any legal challenges in future.

---

# 8. Assistance with Protection (Patents/Trademarks)

Upon Company request, the Assignor agrees to assist in:

- Filing patents  
- Filing trademarks  
- Defending IP ownership  
- Executing additional documents required for legal filings  

The Company covers all legal expenses.

---

# 9. No Compensation Other Than Equity/Agreement

The Assignor acknowledges:

- This assignment is part of their role as founder/employee/contractor.  
- The only compensation is what is defined in the Founders Agreement or employment contract.

They cannot ask for additional compensation for IP contributions.

---

# 10. Return of Materials

Upon separation from the Company, the Assignor must immediately return:

- Devices  
- Documents  
- Access credentials  
- Code, repositories, backups  
- Designs, files, and all confidential data  

No copies may be retained.

---

# 11. Non-Compete & Non-Use of IP

For **3 years after leaving**, Assignor must NOT:

- Build or join a competing platform  
- Reuse Company algorithms or code  
- Approve or assist competitors  
- Use any Company trade secrets  
- Develop derivative products  

This is critical given the confidential and foundational nature of the platform.

---

# 12. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes arising under this Agreement shall be resolved by:

1. Good-faith negotiation  
2. Mediation  
3. Binding arbitration in **[City, Country]**  

Litigation only occurs if legally required.

---

This Agreement is governed by the laws of **[Country/State]**.
